Dogs

Dogs, scientifically known as Canis familiaris, are one of the earliest domesticated animals and have lived alongside humans for thousands of years. They come in many different breeds, each with its own appearance, behavior, and personality. Dogs can serve many roles such as family companions, working animals, guard dogs, and therapy animals. Their characteristics depend on both their genetics and how they are raised, trained, and cared for.

Another breed is the West Highland White Terrier (often called “Westie”). This small but energetic dog comes from Scotland and is easily recognized by its white coat and alert expression. Dachshund

A third example is the Dachshund, sometimes called the “sausage dog” because of its long body and short legs. Dachshunds were originally bred in Germany to hunt animals in burrows, which explains their bold and curious nature.
